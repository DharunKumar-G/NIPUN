# Remedial Reading Camps — 6-Week Intensive

**Program:** Remedial Reading Camps (aligned with NIPUN Bharat FLN targets)
**Source:** NCERT FLN (Foundational Literacy and Numeracy) guidelines, 2021; Pratham Annual Report 2022
**Target grades:** Grade 1–3 (primary); Grade 4–5 (catch-up track)
**Subject:** Reading (letter → word → paragraph progression)
**Estimated cost:** ₹600–900 per child (materials + facilitator honorarium)
**Typical duration:** 6 weeks, 5 days/week, 90 minutes/session

---

## What It Does

Six-week camps run in the school premises after regular hours or during a designated "Foundational Hour." Children who tested at "nothing" or "letter" level on the oral reading assessment are grouped into batches of 12–15. Each session follows the NIPUN Bharat structured pedagogy sequence: phonemic awareness (clapping syllables, rhyme identification), letter-sound mapping using devanagari phonics cards, blending practice, and oral reading of decodable word lists.

## Evidence

NCERT's 2022 FLN evaluation across 6 Hindi-belt states found that schools running structured remedial reading camps (minimum 30 hours over 6 weeks) showed a 14–18 percentage point increase in Grade 2 children reaching "paragraph" level, compared to 4–6 points in control schools. Bihar's NIPUN implementation report (2022–23) cited similar gains in Muzaffarpur and Vaishali districts where camps ran with weekly BEO monitoring.

## What the DEO Does

Identify the 10 lowest reading schools from the Priority Queue. Issue a written order to BEOs in those blocks to run camps in the next academic fortnight. Materials are sourced from NCERT's free FLN print package (available from district resource centre). One para-teacher per school is designated as camp facilitator, paid from the school's composite grant.

## Success Metrics

- Target: 50% of camp children advance one reading level within 6 weeks
- BEO photo-verification of attendance at week 2; mini-test at week 6
