# NIPUN Bharat Structured Pedagogy

**Program:** NIPUN Bharat — National Initiative for Proficiency in Reading with Understanding and Numeracy
**Source:** Ministry of Education, Government of India, 2021; NCERT FLN Implementation Framework 2021
**Target grades:** Grade 1–3 (primary NIPUN targets); Grade 4–5 (catch-up)
**Subject:** Reading and foundational math
**Estimated cost:** Zero incremental cost (centrally funded; materials available from DIET free of charge)
**Typical duration:** Full academic year; structured daily 2-hour "Foundational Literacy and Numeracy" block

---

## What It Does

NIPUN Bharat is the Government of India's flagship FLN mission, launched July 2021, with the goal that every child achieves grade-appropriate literacy and numeracy by Grade 3 by 2026–27. For the DEO, NIPUN Bharat means three things: (1) a structured daily timetable with a dedicated 2-hour FLN block, (2) NCERT-developed FLN print kits (story cards, number cards, activity workbooks) available free from DIET, and (3) a teacher training sequence delivered through DIKSHA (the national edtech platform). NIPUN targets are the yardstick: Grade 3 reading = can read Grade 2-level paragraph; Grade 3 math = can solve 2-digit subtraction.

## Evidence

Early implementation data from 12 states (MoE Progress Report, Dec 2022) showed schools where NIPUN structured timetable was implemented with fidelity had Grade 3 reading gains of 8–14 percentage points at mid-year assessment versus schools with nominal adoption. ASER 2022 (released Jan 2023) noted that Hindi-belt states that ran NIPUN training intensives in 2021–22 showed measurable Grade 3 math recovery from COVID learning loss, particularly in subtraction (the hardest ASER math task for Grade 3).

## What the DEO Does

Use the Priority Queue to identify 10 schools where Grade 3 reading or math is more than 15 percentage points below state average. Issue a written instruction to BEO: these schools must implement the NIPUN daily timetable with fidelity. BEO verifies during school visit using the NIPUN classroom observation checklist (available from NCERT). No budget required — materials come from DIET.

## Success Metrics

- NIPUN timetable fidelity verified in all 10 schools within 4 weeks (BEO checklist)
- ASER-format oral assessment at 3 months: target 50% of below-level children advance one level
- DEO reports progress in next monthly DM review using this app's Monthly Review Builder
