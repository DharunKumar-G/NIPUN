# TaRL — Teaching at the Right Level

**Program:** Teaching at the Right Level (TaRL)
**Source:** Pratham Education Foundation; evaluated by J-PAL (Abdul Latif Jameel Poverty Action Lab)
**Target grades:** Grade 3–5 (primary focus on children reading below grade level)
**Subject:** Reading and foundational math
**Estimated cost:** ₹800–1,200 per child per remedial camp cycle (6 weeks)
**Typical duration:** 6-week intensive camp; 2 hours daily

---

## What It Does

TaRL groups children by their current learning level — not their enrolled grade — and teaches them foundational skills they have actually missed. A Grade 4 child reading at "letter" level is taught in the same group as a Grade 2 child at the same level. Teachers use activity-based tools: picture cards, number lines, stories printed at word-level, dice games.

## Evidence

J-PAL randomized evaluations in Bihar and UP (Pratham, 2001–2007) showed 20–30 percentage point gains in Grade 3 reading within a single academic year. A 2019 replication in Maharashtra (Banerjee, Duflo et al.) confirmed effects persist with government teachers when camp delivery is structured and monitored. ASER 2022 data shows states that ran TaRL-aligned FLN camps in 2021–22 show measurably faster recovery from COVID learning loss than those that did not.

## What the DEO Does

Send BEO to the 20 lowest schools. BEO identifies children at "nothing" and "letter" level using the ASER-style oral assessment tool (15 minutes per class). Groups of 15–20 children are pulled for a 2-hour daily camp, run by a trained Pratham volunteer or a retrained para-teacher. DEO funds 6 weeks of materials (₹800/child) from the school composite grant.

## Success Metrics

- Target: 60% of "letter"-level children reach "word" level within 6 weeks
- BEO submits mini-test score at week 3 and week 6 via this app
