# Principal Leadership Training

**Program:** School Leadership and Management Training (SLMT) — FLN Focus Module
**Source:** NCERT National Programme for School Leadership Development (NPSLD); NIEPA (National Institute of Educational Planning and Administration) Principal Training Framework; ASER 2022 school management findings
**Target grades:** All (principal affects whole-school climate)
**Subject:** Reading and math (whole-school improvement)
**Estimated cost:** ₹1,500–2,500 per principal (3-day residential at DIET); ₹0 for in-school mentoring option
**Typical duration:** 3-day training; 3-month follow-up with monthly BEO visit

---

## What It Does

ASER data consistently shows that teacher attendance — the single strongest predictor of learning outcomes in ASER surveys — varies enormously across schools in the same block. The difference is often the principal. A principal who tracks teacher attendance, gives structured feedback, runs a weekly staff meeting with learning goals, and personally monitors the FLN block creates a school that performs 15–20 percentage points higher than a neighboring school with the same infrastructure but passive leadership. This training gives principals a specific toolkit: how to run a 15-minute Monday morning learning goal-setting meeting, how to use the NIPUN classroom observation checklist themselves, how to handle a teacher who is consistently absent without confrontation.

## Evidence

NIEPA's 2019 evaluation of the NPSLD program across 10 states found schools with trained principals showed significantly higher teacher attendance rates (8 percentage points) and better student assessment scores (11 percentage points on oral reading) at 12 months compared to controls. ASER 2022 qualitative annexure notes that Himachal Pradesh's consistently top-quartile performance is partly attributed to an active SCERT principal mentoring program running since 2013. Note: principal training has longer lag times than direct student interventions; expect results at 6–12 months, not 6 weeks.

## What the DEO Does

Nominate the principals of the 5 lowest-performing schools (as identified in the Priority Queue) for the next DIET leadership training cohort. Coordinate dates with DIET principal. After training, assign BEO to do a "learning walk" with the principal at 30 days and 90 days — a structured 30-minute classroom observation done jointly by BEO and principal, with feedback conversation. Record outcomes in the Intervention Tracker.

## Success Metrics

- Principals complete 3-day DIET training within 6 weeks of nomination
- Teacher attendance rate in target schools improves by 5+ percentage points at 3-month BEO check
- Grade 3 oral reading assessment at 6 months compared to baseline
