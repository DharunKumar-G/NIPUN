# Mother-Tongue Learning Materials

**Program:** Mother-Tongue Based Multilingual Education (MTB-MLE) — print materials
**Source:** NCERT National Curriculum Framework 2023 (MTB-MLE chapter); NEP 2020 Section 4.11; UNESCO 2016 review of MTB-MLE programs in South Asia
**Target grades:** Grade 1–3 (critical window for first-language literacy)
**Subject:** Reading (foundational)
**Estimated cost:** ₹300–500 per child (state-language bridge materials printing)
**Typical duration:** Full academic year; works alongside standard curriculum

---

## What It Does

In Bihar, UP, and Northeast states, many children enter school speaking a home language that differs from the medium of instruction. A child who speaks Bhojpuri at home but is taught in standard Hindi faces a language barrier before a reading barrier. MTB-MLE materials provide a "bridge" — storybooks and decodable readers in the child's home language for Grade 1 and early Grade 2, transitioning to Hindi/English as the child gains confidence. NCERT developed bridge materials for 22 scheduled languages under NEP 2020; Nagaland and Mizoram have state-developed versions.

## Evidence

UNESCO's 2016 South Asia review found MTB-MLE programs in India (Odisha, Andhra Pradesh, Assam) raised Grade 3 reading comprehension by 12–20 percentage points versus comparison schools using Hindi-only instruction. Nagaland's state MTB-MLE program, in place since 2015, is cited in ASER 2022 as a contributing factor to Nagaland's above-average Northeast reading performance despite low infrastructure. NEP 2020 mandates mother-tongue instruction through Grade 5 — DEOs in multilingual districts can cite this policy mandate when requesting materials from the state.

## What the DEO Does

Map the 5 lowest-reading schools against known home-language demographics (check with block education office records). Request MTB-MLE print materials from SCERT (State Council of Educational Research and Training) — these are centrally funded under NEP 2020 implementation. Issue school order to use bridge materials during the 30-minute reading block for Grade 1 and Grade 2.

## Success Metrics

- Materials delivered to target schools within 6 weeks
- Grade 1 oral reading assessment at 3 months and 6 months
- Track: % children reaching "word" level (from "nothing") compared to previous year cohort
