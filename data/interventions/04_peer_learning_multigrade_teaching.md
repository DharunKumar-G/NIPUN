# Peer Learning and Multi-Grade Teaching

**Program:** Peer Learning / Multi-Grade Teaching (adapted from Pratham and NCERT FLN models)
**Source:** Pratham Annual Report 2019; NCERT Multi-Grade Multi-Level (MGML) guidelines
**Target grades:** Grade 3–5 (mixed-level groups)
**Subject:** Reading and math
**Estimated cost:** ₹200–400 per child (materials only; no additional teacher cost)
**Typical duration:** Ongoing classroom restructure; 30-minute peer session daily

---

## What It Does

In schools where a single teacher handles multiple grades (common in rural Bihar, UP, and Northeast states), standard grade-wise instruction is impossible. Peer learning restructures the classroom: children who have reached "story" reading level or "division" math level are trained as "peer tutors" for the 30-minute foundational skills block. The advanced child leads a group of 4–5 peers at a lower level, working through level-appropriate NCERT FLN activity cards. The teacher circulates and monitors. This requires no extra staff and works in single-teacher schools.

## Evidence

NCERT's MGML evaluation in Karnataka and Andhra Pradesh (reported in NCERT Educational Research 2016) found peer-tutored multi-grade groups showed comparable literacy gains to same-grade instruction. Pratham's 2019 Annual Report documents peer learning as a core TaRL delivery mechanism in states with high pupil-teacher ratios (Bihar PTR: 50:1 in some blocks). ASER 2022 notes rural schools with active peer learning structures show higher Grade 5 reading scores even controlling for teacher availability.

## What the DEO Does

Identify 5 single-teacher schools in the priority list. Instruct BEO to run a half-day orientation for the school head teacher: how to identify peer tutors, how to structure the 30-minute session, how to use NCERT FLN activity cards (available free from DIET). Materials budget: ₹200 per school from composite grant.

## Success Metrics

- Peer session running in 5 target schools within 3 weeks (BEO verification)
- Mini-test at 3 months: 40% of tutored children advance one reading level
